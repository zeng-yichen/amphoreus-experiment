### Industry 101

That is traditional construction: everyone uses different, disconnected software, leading to wasted materials, blown budgets, and massive delays. 

"Connected Construction" is like giving everyone on the project a universal, real-time translation earpiece and a single shared blueprint. It brings design, field execution, and financial data under one roof so the COO and CFO can actually see what is happening in real-time, stop wasting money, and complete projects faster with fewer workers.

### The Jargon Cheat Sheet
*   **AECO:** Architecture, Engineering, Construction, and Operations. The broad acronym for the entire industry lifecycle Trimble serves.
*   **Point Solution:** A piece of software designed to solve one specific problem (e.g., just scheduling or just 3D modeling). Trimble is actively moving *away* from selling these individually.
*   **BIM (Building Information Modeling):** The foundation of digital construction; intelligent 3D modeling software used by architects and engineers to plan and manage buildings.
*   **CDE (Common Data Environment):** A single, centralized source of truth for collecting, managing, and sharing all project data across different teams.
*   **WIP (Work in Progress) Reporting:** A crucial financial accounting metric for construction CFOs to track project costs, billing, and profitability in real-time.
*   **Digital Twin:** A virtual, real-time replica of a physical building or infrastructure asset used to simulate, predict, and optimize operations and maintenance.
