### Athos Therapeutics
**1. Target Overview**
Athos Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biotechnology company based in Los Angeles, CA. They specialize in developing precision small-molecule therapeutics for immune-mediated and autoimmune diseases, primarily Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD), Ulcerative Colitis, and Crohn's Disease. Their core differentiator is **AthosOmics.AI**, a proprietary, no-code AI platform that integrates multi-omic patient data (genomics, transcriptomics, microbiome) to predict mechanisms of action and design first-in-class drugs.

*   **March 2026:** CMO Dr. Allan Pantuck presented highly successful Phase 1 data at the 2026 Crohn's and Colitis Congress, noting zero serious adverse events. He announced the Phase 2 trial will span North America, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East.
*   **November 2025:** Announced a strategic partnership with Xeptiva Therapeutics to utilize the AthosOmics.AI platform for discovering vaccine biomarkers, proving they are actively monetizing their AI data platform alongside their drug pipeline.

**3. Predicted Pain Points**
*   **The Multi-Center, International Phase 2 Leap:** Transitioning from a controlled Phase 1 safety study to an international Phase 2 trial across North America, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East introduces massive statistical noise. They will face challenges with site-to-site variability, regional standard-of-care differences, and complex mixed-model statistical requirements.
*   **Translating AI to FDA-Approved Endpoints:** Athos relies heavily on AI-derived multi-omic data. Translating these complex, AI-predicted biomarkers into a rigorous, traditional Statistical Analysis Plan (SAP) that satisfies the FDA's strict efficacy requirements is a delicate balancing act. 
*   **M&A Due Diligence Preparation:** As Mark noted in Transcript 5, startups running Phase 2 trials often look to sell the asset to Big Pharma (like Johnson & Johnson or Roche) for Phase 3. Athos needs bulletproof, unassailable statistical evidence to survive the rigorous due diligence of a Big Pharma acquisition.
