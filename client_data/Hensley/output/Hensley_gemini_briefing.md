### The Jargon Cheat Sheet
*   **SAP (Statistical Analysis Plan):** The foundational, highly detailed document required by the FDA/EMA that dictates exactly how clinical trial data will be analyzed.
*   **Effect Size:** The magnitude of the difference between the treatment group and the control group (the "black art" is estimating this for humans based on animal or cell models).
*   **Non-Inferiority Trial:** A trial designed to prove a new treatment is *not unacceptably worse* than an existing predicate device or drug (very common in medtech).
*   **HDE (Humanitarian Device Exemption):** An FDA regulatory pathway for rare diseases where patient populations are too small for traditional, large-scale statistical models.
*   **CDISC / SDTM:** Strict, standardized data formatting requirements mandated by the FDA for clinical submissions. 
*   **Power Calculation:** The mathematical process of determining the minimum sample size needed to detect a statistically significant effect in a trial.
*   **RWE (Real-World Evidence):** Post-market clinical data used to prove real-world effectiveness, usually to convince insurance companies (Medicare/Medicaid) to reimburse the product.
