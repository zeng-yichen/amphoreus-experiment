Getting FDA approval is the easy part. Integrating insurance reimbursement into your clinical program from the beginning - that's the larger challenge.

Most biotech founders celebrate FDA approval as the finish line.
It's not. It's halftime.

Because even with FDA approval, if there is too much of a delay in UnitedHealthcare, Cigna, and Medicare reimbursing your treatment, you miss out on profits and investors aren't going to be happy.

Here's the thing: most founders know this. Insurance reimbursement isn't some blind spot. They're aware of it and actively thinking about it. They just haven't planned for it with the same rigor they bring to their clinical program.

After your clinical trial shows your device or drug works in a controlled setting, payers want to know:

→ Does it actually work in real-world clinical practice?
→ Is it cost-effective compared to existing treatments?
→ Do patients actually use it correctly outside of trial conditions?
→ What's the real-world safety profile across diverse populations?

The typical workflow we see:

Phase 1-3 trials → FDA approval → Celebration → Reimbursement strategy cobbled together after the fact

The smarter workflow:

Phase 1-3 trials → FDA approval → Post-marketing RWE study already designed → faster insurance reimbursement approval 

Why RWE studies are statistically harder than clinical trials:

In clinical trials: Clean randomization. Controlled patient populations. Clear inclusion/exclusion criteria. In RWE: Hospital administrative records. No control over patient demographics. Messier data. More complex statistical models needed.

What we help clients build for payer negotiations:

Real-world effectiveness data (not just efficacy). Cost-benefit analyses showing value add. Quality-of-life improvements beyond clinical endpoints. 

The companies that get reimbursement faster aren't the ones who suddenly discover they need an RWE strategy post-approval. They're the ones who had it mapped out before they submitted to the FDA. They design their Phase 3 trials with RWE collection in mind. They build relationships with hospital systems early. They plan their post-marketing surveillance alongside their regulatory submission.
