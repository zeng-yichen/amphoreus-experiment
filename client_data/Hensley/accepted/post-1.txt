I'm a biostatistician who tells clients NOT to buy more than they need from us.

Here's the service I sometimes talk clients out of: The 50-page Statistical Analysis Plan. Before you come at me - I know. For larger, more complicated trials, you need it. Regulatory requirement, I get it. But for simpler trials, you're paying us to reword large chunks of your protocol.

The better approach: An abbreviated SAP focused on statistical methods, analysis populations, missing data handling, and interim analysis plans. Everything else? Reference the protocol, don't rehash it.

Examples of where you DO need the full 50-page SAP: Multi-national pharma trials. Regulatory jurisdictions that explicitly require it. Complex adaptive designs with multiple interim looks. Your CRO's SOPs demand it. 

I make money writing these documents, and I'm telling you: most early stage founders don't need them. Because my goal isn't maximizing our revenue, it's helping you allocate resources where they'll have the biggest impact. Sometimes that means telling you to spend less.

The clients who appreciate this honesty become repeat customers, and they refer everyone they know. That's worth more than one inflated SAP invoice.
