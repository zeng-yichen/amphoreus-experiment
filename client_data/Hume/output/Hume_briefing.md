### The Three-Stage Pain Lifecycle

**Stage 1: Architecture ("Can we even build this?")**
- Voice AI has a unique architectural pattern for how text and voice interact with GPUs, models, and tokens
- Getting this to work at all—before any data—is hard. Scaling it is even harder.
- The engineers tasked with this work would rather be doing "sexier" things

**Stage 2: Training & Emotional Nuance ("Does it sound human?")**
- Multiple languages, 18+ Indian dialects, Japanese, Swedish, Mandarin—requires millions of hours of data
- Beyond basic comprehension: a speaker can be happy, pissed off, AND sad simultaneously—affecting tone, rhythm, timbre, pause patterns
- Hume maps 600-800+ granular emotional labels using Semantic Space Theory
- Current alternatives: scrape and process yourself, use Scale AI/Mechanical Turk/label farms (expensive, low quality for audio), or buy from a specialist
- The 12-page PDF instruction problem: even educated people can't label well with bad instructions

**Stage 3: Evaluation & Production ("Does it stay good?")**
- Models degrade or behave unexpectedly in production
- A bad text response is forgettable; a bad voice response is visceral and trust-destroying
- Continuous measurement required for drift detection and quality assurance
- No standardized benchmarks exist for "conversational naturalness"—Hume is building them
- Hume has an internal-turned-external eval API: developers invoke human evaluators through code, get results back in hours, with no meetings. Already secured a multi-million-dollar commitment.

### Psychological Triggers
