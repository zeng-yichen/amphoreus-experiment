1. Sesame (San Francisco) 

They're building voice companions with 200-300ms response times and emotional cues integrated into lightweight smart glasses.

What they're doing differently:
• They process audio and text together (unlike the standard STT pipelines)
• Beta voices "Maya" and "Miles" already generated 5M+ minutes of interaction
• 51-200 employees, mostly Oculus alumni who know how to ship hardware

What sets them apart: interactions that resemble a friendly conversation rather than speaking to a machine.

2. Hippocratic AI (Palo Alto)

The team's mission centers on developing safety-first AI agents for healthcare that interacts with patients, with voice interactions that are empathetic.

Some things worth highlighting:
• Non-diagnostic, clinically safe AI that focuses on patient engagement
• "AI Agent App Store" for third-party healthcare developers
• Pilots starting at 25-100 agents with major health systems

Healthcare is the highest stakes proving ground for emotional intelligence in voice AI. You are having conversations with patients that need help - the UX can't fail.

3. Bland AI (San Francisco)

They're automating enterprise phone calls with hyper-realistic AI agents that handle 1M+ simultaneous calls.

The real differentiator:
• 24/7 operations with multi-language support
• Customers include Cleveland Cavaliers and Better.com
• Went from stealth to Series B < 10 months

They have been able to execute with quality of the conversation... which is rare.

4. Cartesia (San Francisco)

Their Sonic 2.0 model hits 90ms latency (40ms turbo mode) and was preferred 1.5x over competitors in blind tests.

What they're building:
• State space model architecture for voice generation
• 99.9% uptime with SOC-2 and HIPAA compliance
• Powers 1B+ calls/month for customers like Forethought

Technical infrastructure that works at scale.
