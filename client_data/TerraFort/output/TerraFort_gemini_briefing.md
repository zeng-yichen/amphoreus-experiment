### The Jargon Cheat Sheet
1.  **EOC (Emergency Operations Center):** The physical or virtual command center where local, state, and federal leaders coordinate disaster response and resource allocation.
2.  **Blue Skies vs. Gray Skies:** "Blue skies" refers to normal, non-disaster times (when government procurement is a slow, bureaucratic nightmare). "Gray skies" refers to an active disaster (when emergency funds are unlocked and leaders can buy tech instantly).
3.  **Sole-Source Contract:** A non-competitive procurement method. During emergencies, governments can bypass the usual 12-to-18-month bidding process to buy TerraFort immediately to protect life and property.
4.  **Damage Assessment / PDA (Preliminary Damage Assessment):** The critical process of quantifying the physical and financial impact of a disaster, which is legally required to trigger federal aid.
5.  **FEMA PA (Public Assistance):** The federal grant program that reimburses local governments and municipalities for disaster recovery (e.g., rebuilding public infrastructure). TerraFort's software helps secure this specific funding.
6.  **Disaster Intelligence / Earth Intelligence:** The use of AI, GIS (mapping), and remote sensing (satellites/drones) to anticipate weather impacts and assess ground damage in real-time.
